<p><ul><li>Chemical Vapor Deposition</li><li>Liquid Impregnation Process</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Reinforced carbon-carbon (RCC) is used in high-temperature applications, particularly in aerospace. The market types include Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process (LIP). CVD involves depositing carbon materials onto a substrate in a gaseous phase, creating a strong matrix. In contrast, LIP entails saturating a pre-formed carbon material with a liquid resin that hardens, enhancing its mechanical properties. Both methods enhance RCC's performance, catering to industries like aerospace, automotive, and energy, where durability and heat resistance are critical.</p></p>
