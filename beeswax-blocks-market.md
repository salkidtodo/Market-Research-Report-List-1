<p><p>Beeswax blocks are categorized into two primary types: Cera Flava and Cera Alba. Cera Flava, or yellow beeswax, is a natural, unrefined product derived from honeycomb and retains the characteristic aroma and color of beeswax. In contrast, Cera Alba, or white beeswax, is bleached and purified, offering a cleaner appearance and milder scent, making it ideal for cosmetics and pharmaceuticals. Both types are widely used in various industries, including skincare, candle making, and food preservation, due to their beneficial properties.</p></p>
